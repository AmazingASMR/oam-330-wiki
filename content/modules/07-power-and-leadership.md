# Module 7 — Power & Leadership

## What this module is fundamentally about

Three questions structure the module (slide 5): **who ends up as a leader, what happens to people when they become leaders, and why do leaders fail?** Leader emergence is mostly about perceived competence and commitment, with cosmetic factors (height, attractiveness, extraversion) at the margin. Once power is acquired, the Behavioral Inhibition System quiets — leaders take more action, feel less pressure from others, and lose perspective-taking; Keltner and Obhi show this is partly neurological (impaired mirroring). The result is the **power paradox**: the very capacities that earned someone power get eroded by having it. A second strand — covered the next class — is **power distance** as a Hofstede cultural dimension, with the KAL 801 / Korean Air case as the canonical illustration of how high power distance combined with mitigated speech can be catastrophic.

## Headline empirical points

- **KAL 801 (Guam, Aug 5, 1997)** — high-PD Korean cockpit; first officer hints "Don't you think it rains more in this area, here?"; engineer hints "the weather radar has helped us a lot." Captain doesn't decode. **228 of 254 die.** Korean Air's loss rate 1988–1998 was **4.79 / million departures** — about **17× United's**. PD-crash correlation **r = .47**.
- **Avianca 052 (Long Island, Jan 1990)** — Colombian crew; FO Klotz says "we're running out of fuel, sir" with mitigating "ah" instead of declaring "emergency." NY ATC dismisses it. Plane crashes from fuel exhaustion.
- **Korean Air reform** — recognized PD as the root cause; introduced training to **reduce mitigated speech** and mandated **English in the cockpit** (a low-PD register). Today: 5-star Skytrax rating.
- **Keltner research** — two decades of behavioral work showing the powerful behave like people with mild traumatic brain injury (more impulsive, less risk-aware, worse at perspective-taking); produces an **empathy deficit** via reduced **mirroring**.
- **Obhi TMS study** — high-power subjects' mirror-neuron pathways fire less when watching rubber-ball videos; conscious effort to compensate (Obhi & Naish) doesn't restore mirroring.
- **Owen & Davidson hubris syndrome (2008/2009, *Brain*)** — proposed clinical disorder of leaders subjected to **sustained power with minimal constraint**; **14 features** including contempt for others, loss of contact with reality, restless/reckless action, displays of incompetence, accountability avoidance.
- **Galinsky's "E on the forehead" study (2006)** — high-power participants **3× more likely** to draw the E self-readable (backwards to others); George W. Bush holding the flag backwards at the 2008 Olympics is the public-life analog.
- **Hofstede's six cultural dimensions** — power distance, individualism-collectivism, uncertainty avoidance, masculinity-femininity, long- vs. short-term orientation, indulgence vs. restraint. **Power distance** is the lens for this module; the KAL chapter ranks pilot PDIs (top: Brazil, S. Korea, Morocco, Mexico, Philippines; bottom: US, Ireland, S. Africa, Australia, NZ).

## Reading-specific testable points

**Gladwell — "The Ethnic Theory of Plane Crashes":**
- Crashes typically involve **seven consecutive human errors of teamwork and communication**, not one big technical failure — usually two pilots who haven't flown together, in poor weather, behind schedule, with a tired captain.
- **Hofstede's 1980 IBM-employee study** is the source of the cultural-dimensions framework Gladwell uses; **Helmreich** built the pilot-PDI ranking on top of it.
- **Receiver-oriented vs. transmitter-oriented communication** — high-PD cultures put the burden on the listener; low-PD cultures put it on the speaker. KAL 801's Korean cockpit was receiver-oriented while talking to a transmitter-oriented US ATC system.
- **Counter-example: Suren Ratwatte's Helsinki diversion** — Sri Lankan captain who freely communicates with ATC, wakes a second crew, negotiates direction-of-landing across 40 minutes — shows what low-PD cockpit speech looks like.
- **Mr. Kim / kwacang dinner exchange** — Korean indirect-offer norm uses **six levels of address**, illustrating how much meaning rides on the receiver in high-PD speech.

**Useem — "Power Causes Brain Damage":**
- **Keltner's brain-damage parallel** — Keltner's two decades of work shows the powerful behave like people with a mild TBI; Obhi's TMS study gives the neural substrate (reduced mirroring).
- **Stanford prison-guard parallel** — sustained power + minimal constraint quickly induces hubristic behavior even in randomly assigned "guards"; analog for the hubris-syndrome onset condition.
- **John Stumpf** at the Wells Fargo fake-account hearing — emergence-time strength (read people) became failure-time weakness ("Cross-selling is shorthand for deepening relationships" — couldn't read the room).
- **Toe holders** (Louis Howe re: FDR; Indra Nooyi's mother — "leave that damn crown in the garage"; Clementine Churchill — "you are not as kind as you used to be") = the social antidote to hubris drift.
- **Average tenure ≈ 4 years** for US presidents-of-power roles is the Useem framing for why hubris syndrome rarely fully consolidates in elected US leaders — short tenure plus checks and balances limits the "sustained power + minimal constraint" precondition.
- **Rau et al., *J. Finance* 2017** — CEOs who lived through fatal natural disasters as children are *less* risk-seeking; non-fatal-disaster CEOs are *more* risk-seeking. Constraint experiences leave lasting marks.

## Common points of confusion

- **Leader emergence vs. leader effectiveness** — emergence = who is *perceived as* leader (driven by perceived competence + commitment, with cosmetic boosters); effectiveness = whether they actually produce good outcomes. **Emergent ≠ effective.** Talkativeness is a *behavior of* emergent leaders, not a primary driver.
- **Power paradox (mechanism) vs. how power changes behavior (effects) vs. hubris syndrome (clinical pattern)** — a three-way trap.
  - Paradox = the *circular self-defeating loop* (capacities that won you power get switched off).
  - Power-changes-behavior = the *three-item behavioral list* (action ↑, perspective-taking ↓, pressure of others ↓), via BIS suppression.
  - Hubris syndrome = the *clinical disorder* requiring **sustained** power + minimal constraint (Owen, 14 features).
  - The paradox shows up in 10-minute priming; hubris syndrome takes years.
- **Power distance describes CULTURES, not individuals** — slide-explicit. An "individual high in power distance" is the wrong framing; the right framing is "an individual from a high-PD culture." Don't conflate PD with individualism-collectivism — both are Hofstede dimensions but PD is about *hierarchy*, I/C is about *group orientation*.
- **High PD ≠ "bad"** — slide 13 explicitly: high PD also brings respect for elders, fewer conflicts, sensitivity to roles, clarity of belonging. There is **no "right way"**.
- **Mitigated speech vs. receiver/transmitter-oriented** — mitigated speech is the *transmitter behavior* (subordinate softens up to a superior); receiver-orientation is the *cultural expectation* that the listener will decode. They co-occur in high-PD cultures but are distinct moves.
- **Mitigated speech vs. Cialdini authority** (M6/M7 cross-module) — mitigated speech = low-status speaker softens *up*; authority = listener over-defers to high-status speaker. Inverse directions of the same hierarchy.
- **Leader-emergence helpfulness** — the slide answer to "is it helpful that a leader emerges?" is **yes, with caveats**: moderate status differences boost coordination/efficiency; large or unfair ones hurt creativity and satisfaction. Distractors: "flat groups always outperform" or "steeper hierarchy always boosts efficiency."
- **Hubris syndrome vs. narcissism** — narcissism is a stable trait *before* power; hubris syndrome is *induced* by sustained power and can recede when power is removed.

## Notes on application
- **KAL 801 / Avianca scenario stems** — cockpit transcript with hints; identify as **mitigated speech** in a **high-PD** cockpit; pair the mechanism with **receiver-oriented** communication. Korean Air's English mandate and mitigated-speech training are the canonical PD-reduction interventions.
- **Keltner-mechanism scenarios** — a recently promoted manager shows reduced perspective-taking, more goal-action, less pressure from subordinates. Identify the trio (action ↑ / perspective ↓ / pressure of others ↓) and the **BIS suppression** mechanism. Identify Keltner / Obhi / mirroring / empathy deficit as keywords. The Stumpf "couldn't read the room" stem and the Galinsky "E on forehead" stem both point here.
- **PD cultural-comparison stems** — match a workplace artifact (CEO parking spot, open door vs. gatekeepers, large vs. small pay spread, flat vs. tall structure, grievance structures vs. no way out, boss-as-friend vs. boss-as-benevolent-parent) to high or low PD. Trap: assuming high PD is universally bad.
- **"Is it helpful that a leader emerges?"** — the slide answer is **yes, with caveats** (moderate status differences help; large/unfair ones hurt). Read the answer choices carefully — the right one will hedge.
- **Hubris-syndrome diagnostic stem** — sustained power + minimal constraint + cluster of behaviors (contempt, restless action, accountability avoidance) → identify Owen's syndrome, distinguish from the general power paradox and from trait narcissism.
- **Toe-holder / antidote stem** — Indra Nooyi's mother, Clementine Churchill, Louis Howe re: FDR — recognize as the **social** prevention of hubris drift; pair with the slide's structural antidotes (transparency, accountability, procedural-justice voice mechanisms).
