## Main argument
Groups are defined by interaction, interdependence, and shared objectives. Their behavior and outcomes are shaped by six properties — roles, norms, status, size, cohesiveness, and diversity — overlaid on a developmental arc (the punctuated equilibrium model for temporary groups). Group decision making has clear strengths (information, diversity, acceptance) and weaknesses (time, conformity, dominance, ambiguous responsibility), and is corrupted by groupthink and groupshift unless deliberately countered.

## Key terms introduced
- **Group**: two or more individuals, interacting and interdependent, who have come together to achieve certain objectives.
- **Formal vs. informal group**: defined by org structure vs. spontaneous social formation.
- **Social identity theory**: emotional reactions to group success/failure because self-esteem is tied to the group.
- **Ingroup / outgroup**: ours vs. theirs; favoritism (not hostility) drives most discrimination.
- **Punctuated equilibrium model**: temporary groups — first-meeting direction → Phase 1 inertia → midpoint transition → Phase 2 inertia → final-meeting burst.
- **Role / role perception / role expectations / psychological contract / role conflict / interrole conflict**: roles cluster.
- **Norms / conformity / reference groups**: norms must be accepted by all to stick; reference groups are those we identify with strongly enough to conform to.
- **Deviant workplace behavior (CWB)**: voluntary norm-violating behavior that threatens the org or its members; categorized as production / property / political / personal aggression.
- **Status / status characteristics theory**: status from power, contribution, or personal characteristics.
- **Social loafing**: less effort in groups than alone (Western/individualist bias).
- **Cohesiveness**: shared bond to work together and stay; productivity = cohesion × performance norms.
- **Diversity / faultlines**: surface vs. functional; faultlines = perceived subgroup splits.
- **Groupthink / groupshift**: conformity-driven appraisal failure / polarization toward extremes.
- **Interacting groups / brainstorming / nominal group technique**: three group decision-making formats; NGT outperforms brainstorming for ideas.

## Examples used
- Asch line study: 75% conformed at least once, avg conformer wrong 37% of the time.
- Hawthorne studies (1924–1932): bank wiring room — group norms restrict output; "rate buster," "chiseler," "squealer" enforced via ridicule.
- Tom's Marine Sales 50th anniversary (social identity).
- Wendy's Twitter "roasting wars" (ingroup/outgroup).
- Independent Group (UK MPs, 2019) and *Ocean's* movies (roles).
- Samsung Galaxy Note 7 recall (groupthink).
- Miriam O'Reilly / BBC age-discrimination case (stigma by association).
