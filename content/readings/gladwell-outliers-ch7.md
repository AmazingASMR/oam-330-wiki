## Main argument
Plane crashes are rarely caused by one big technical failure; they typically involve seven consecutive human errors of *teamwork and communication*, often in cockpits with two pilots who have never flown together, in poor weather, behind schedule, with a tired captain. When the first officer's culture has a high power-distance index (PDI), they hint instead of asserting — "mitigated speech" — and the captain misses it. Korean Air's loss rate (4.79 / million departures, 1988–1998) was 17× United's. The fix that turned KAL into a 5-star airline was reducing power distance in the cockpit: training away mitigated speech and requiring English (a low-PD register) for cockpit communication.

## Key terms introduced
- **Power Distance Index (PDI)** — Hofstede's measure of how much a culture accepts unequal distribution of power. Top pilot PDIs: Brazil, S. Korea, Morocco, Mexico, Philippines. Bottom: US, Ireland, S. Africa, Australia, NZ.
- **Mitigated speech** — downplayed, hint-laden communication of concerns to a superior; the typical first-officer mode in high-PD cockpits.
- **Receiver-oriented communication** — listener must decode meaning (Korean/Asian norm); contrast with **transmitter-oriented** Western norm where speaker is responsible for clarity.
- **Hofstede's Dimensions** — broader framework: individualism-collectivism (US highest, Guatemala lowest), uncertainty avoidance (Greece highest, Singapore lowest), and PDI.

## Examples used
- **KAL 801, Guam, August 5, 1997** — captain commits to visual approach with no backup; first officer hints "Don't you think it rains more in this area, here?"; engineer hints "the weather radar has helped us a lot." Captain doesn't decode. Hits Nimitz Hill. 228 of 254 die.
- **Avianca 052, Long Island, January 1990** — Colombian crew running out of fuel; first officer Klotz says "we're running out of fuel, sir" with mitigating "ah" instead of declaring "emergency." NY ATC dismisses. Plane crashes from fuel exhaustion. After last exchange Klotz tells captain: "the guy is angry."
- **Suren Ratwatte's Helsinki diversion** — counterexample: Sri Lankan captain freely talks to ATC, wakes second crew, negotiates direction-of-landing. Communicates throughout 40 minutes.
- **Mr. Kim / kwacang dinner exchange** — illustrates Korean indirect-offer linguistic norm (six levels of address).
