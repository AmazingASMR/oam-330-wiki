## Main argument
Thinking errors are **systematic, not random**, and they are most dangerous precisely when they feel most like clear thinking. Dobelli's two assigned chapters cover **two backward-looking biases** that both stem from over-weighting prior information:
- **Sunk costs** trap us in failing courses of action because abandoning them would force us to admit a past decision was wrong (consistency motive).
- **Hindsight** reshapes memory so past events look inevitable, inflating our confidence as forecasters and shielding us from the corrective feedback that miscalibration should produce.

Dobelli's recurring move: awareness alone doesn't fix these biases ("studies have shown that people who are aware of it fall for it just as much as everyone else"), so the solution is **structural** — keep a diary, use reference distributions, separate decisions from sunk costs in writing.

## Key terms introduced
- **Sunk cost fallacy** — over-weighting non-recoverable past investments when choosing a next step.
- **Concorde effect** — alias for sunk cost; supersonic-jet failure that Britain & France kept funding to save face.
- **Need for consistency** — the psychological engine of sunk-cost behavior; we hate admitting our prior selves were wrong.
- **Hindsight bias** — "I told you so" / "knew it all along"; reconstructing past beliefs to match known outcomes.
- **Curse of knowledge** (implicit) — once you know an answer, you can't recover the perspective of not knowing it.

## Examples used
- **Movie example** — Dobelli vs. wife: she won't leave a bad movie because they "already paid $30." He explains the fallacy; she stays anyway.
- **Marketing campaign** at his firm: 4 months in, missing every goal — the manager wants to keep going "because we've invested so much."
- **Friend's relationship** — staying with a serial cheater because of "energy invested."
- **Investors** — refusing to sell losing stocks because of acquisition price; ironically holding losers harder the more they fall.
- **Concorde** (UK + France), **Vietnam War** extension — government-scale sunk costs.
- **Hindsight: great-uncle's diary** in occupied Paris (1940) — predicted Germans would leave by year-end; occupation lasted 4 years. Diaries reveal the actual uncertainty hindsight erases.
- **2007 economists** — rosy on the economy; after the 2008 crash the "obvious" causes (Greenspan, lax mortgages, rating agencies) appeared.
- **1980 Reagan landslide / 1998 Google / 1914 Sarajevo** — all retrospectively "inevitable," none predicted.
