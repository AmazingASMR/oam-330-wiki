## Definition
**Workplace stigmatization** = the devaluing of your identity at work. It ranges from extreme hostility (e.g., quid pro quo sexual harassment) to everyday incivilities (jokes about your religion, co-workers who avoid eye contact).

## Why it matters
Two consequences appear on the slides:
1. **Job satisfaction / turnover** — interpersonal relationships drive job satisfaction; no one wants to work where they feel unwelcome or disrespected.
2. **Performance** — stigmatization hurts performance both directly and through stereotype threat and identity-concealment cost.

Prevalence statistics from the slide:
- ~3/4 of employees report workplace incivility in the last year.
- ~50% of women will report sexual harassment during their careers.
- 10–20% of racial minorities experienced discrimination in their *current* job.

A direct respect → performance link: racial-minority salespeople show highest sales in branches whose manager reported valuing diversity.

## Key distinctions
- **Stigmatization vs. discrimination** — discrimination is unequal treatment (often hiring/promotion); stigmatization is devaluing of identity through everyday interaction. They overlap but the OB lens here is interpersonal, not legal.
- **Stigmatization vs. stereotype threat** — stigmatization is what *others* do to you; stereotype threat is the *internal* performance hit from identity salience (next page).
- **Visible vs. invisible identities** — visible identities are stigmatized "all day"; invisible ones face the disclose-or-conceal cost.

## Example(s)
Co-workers who won't make eye contact; jokes about religion; sexual harassment.

## Likely MCQ angles
Definition (devaluing of identity); the *range* of behaviors (extreme hostility through everyday incivility); both satisfaction *and* performance consequences.
