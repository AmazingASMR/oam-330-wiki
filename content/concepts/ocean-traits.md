## Definition
The Big 5 dimensions, mnemonic **OCEAN**:
- **Openness to experience** — degree to which someone is curious, imaginative, and drawn to novelty, ideas, and aesthetics versus conventional and concrete.
- **Conscientiousness** — degree to which someone is organized, dependable, persistent, and self-disciplined versus careless and impulsive.
- **Extraversion** — degree to which someone is outgoing, energetic, and sociable versus reserved and quiet.
- **Agreeableness** — degree to which someone is warm, cooperative, and trusting versus competitive and antagonistic.
- **Neuroticism** — degree to which someone experiences negative emotions (anxiety, anger, sadness) and reacts strongly to stress versus emotional stability (a.k.a. emotional (in)stability or negative emotionality).

## Why it matters
Everyone has a continuous score on every trait — no "type." This is the empirically grounded vocabulary of personality, and the basis for predicting job and life outcomes ([[concepts/ocean-job-performance]]).

## Key distinctions
- **vs. MBTI dichotomies** — Big 5 traits are continuous percentile scores; MBTI forces a binary cut at the midpoint, destroying information ([[concepts/myers-briggs]]).
- **Neuroticism ≠ clinical neurosis** — it's a normal-range tendency toward negative affect, not pathology (Gosling's caveat).
- **Five, not four** — frequent distractor: students drop a letter or substitute "honesty" (HEXACO).

## Example(s)
- Da Vinci as openness icon; Robocop as conscientiousness icon (Gosling).
- Williams's class profile: mean scores near the 45th–58th percentile across all five — wide range (1st–99th).
