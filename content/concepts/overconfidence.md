## Definition
We are **wildly overconfident** in predicting our own outcomes — abilities, knowledge, future performance. Our subjective certainty greatly exceeds our actual accuracy. Slides bucket overconfidence under **"we want to think well of ourselves."**

## Why it matters
Calibration failure across nearly every domain. Couples with [[concepts/hindsight-bias]] to make us feel like better forecasters than we are, leading to excess risk-taking and underestimated timelines. Kahneman ("Don't Blink") frames it as a hazard of expert judgment — confidence and accuracy are nearly uncorrelated.

## Sub-effects covered in slides
- **Dunning-Kruger effect:** we are **MORE** overconfident in domains where we have **LESS** experience or skill. Counterintuitive — novices feel more certain than experts.
- **Planning fallacy:** predictions made on **best-case scenarios**, not likely ones — and based on *this specific task*, not the average of past tasks of this type. Linked to [[readings/freakonomics-planning-fallacy]].
- **Better-than-average effect (slide demo, N=63):** OAM 330 students rated own niceness 3.9, driving 3.6, academic ability 3.8 (all above the 3.0 midpoint = above the median classmate). Statistically impossible at scale.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. [[concepts/hindsight-bias]]** — overconfidence is *forward*-looking ("I'll predict the future correctly"); hindsight bias is *backward*-looking ("I would have predicted that outcome"). Hindsight feeds overconfidence by inflating perceived past accuracy.
- **vs. [[concepts/halo-effects]]** — halo distorts judgments of *others*; overconfidence distorts judgments of *self*.

## Example(s)
- Raiffa & Alpert / Dobelli: 98%-confidence ranges are right far less than 98% of the time.
- Buehler's planning fallacy studies: even at 99% confidence, fewer than half of student projects finished on time.

## Likely MCQ angles
Dunning-Kruger direction (less skill → MORE overconfidence — students often invert this). Best-case-scenario framing of planning fallacy. Above-average self-ratings demo.
