## Definition
The two **alternative study designs** when a true experiment isn't possible (slide 15):

- **Quasi-experimental study** — takes advantage of a **natural or existing division**: different companies, branches, floors, classes, or the timing of a new policy. The "groups" exist on their own; the experimenter doesn't randomly assign.
- **Correlational study** — **measures** the relationship between variables (instead of manipulating them). E.g., # of customers served per day and customer satisfaction.

## Why it matters
These designs are necessary when [[concepts/experiment-obstacles|obstacles to experimentation]] block an RCT. They sit *below* RCTs on the [[concepts/evidence-hierarchy]] because neither has [[concepts/random-assignment]] — so neither establishes causation cleanly. They lean heavily on [[concepts/control-variables]] to rule out alternatives.

## Key distinctions
- **RCT** — experimenter manipulates IV + randomly assigns to conditions. Causation possible.
- **Quasi-experiment** — uses pre-existing groups (e.g., before/after a new policy at one branch vs. another). No random assignment, but there *are* discrete groups/conditions.
- **Correlational study** — no group assignment at all; just measure two variables and compute the association.
- **Quasi vs. correlational** — quasi has a defined "treatment" (a natural division acts like one); correlational has only continuous or measured variables, no treatment.

## Example(s)
- **Quasi**: a company rolls out a new wellness policy at the Atlanta branch but not the Boston branch — you compare outcomes across branches.
- **Correlational**: measure how many customers a server handles per day and rate customer satisfaction; compute the correlation.

## Likely MCQ angles
- Match a study description to design type. **Trap:** "different branches" with no manipulation = quasi-experiment, not RCT.
- Recognize that neither design supports causation.
- Why control variables matter for non-experimental designs (you have no random assignment to do the work for you).
