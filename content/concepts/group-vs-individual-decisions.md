## Definition
A comparison of when group decision making outperforms individual decision making and vice versa, accounting for accuracy, speed, creativity, acceptance, and efficiency.

## Why it matters
Managers must choose the right decision mode. Groups bring more information and acceptance; individuals are faster and avoid conformity costs.

## Strengths of group decisions
- More complete information and knowledge (pooled resources).
- Increased diversity of views / more alternatives considered.
- Increased acceptance of the solution by participants.

## Weaknesses of group decisions
- Time-consuming.
- Conformity pressures (groupthink risk).
- Discussion can be dominated by one or a few members.
- Ambiguous responsibility — accountability is diluted.

## Effectiveness vs. efficiency
- Group decisions are generally MORE accurate than the average individual but LESS accurate than the BEST individual.
- Individuals beat groups on **speed**.
- Groups beat individuals on **creativity** and **acceptance**.
- Groups are generally LESS efficient (more total work-hours).

## Key distinctions
- **Groupthink** — a *failure mode* of group decision making, not the same as group decision making itself.
- **Groupshift / polarization** — distinct phenomenon: groups push initial leanings toward more extreme positions.
- **Common-knowledge effect** — a specific failure where shared info dominates discussion at the expense of unique private info.

## Example(s)
- Slides (Grogan Air exercise): with full info, 100% pick Janet; with divided info, ~75% pick Katherine — groups under-leverage private info.
- Juries: classic "two heads are better than one" assumption.
