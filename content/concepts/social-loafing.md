## Definition
The tendency for individuals to expend less effort when working collectively than when working individually.

## Why it matters
Directly challenges the assumption that group productivity equals the sum of individuals' productivity. Bigger groups → more loafing → diminishing returns on adding members for productive (not idea-generating) tasks.

## Key distinctions
- **Free-riding (economics)** — overlapping but loafing is the OB term and emphasizes effort reduction specifically because of collective context.
- **Groupthink** — groupthink is conformity-driven suppression of dissent; loafing is effort reduction. Both are group dysfunctions but mechanistically distinct.
- **Production blocking** (in brainstorming) — loafing is willful effort reduction; production blocking is cognitive interference from many people talking at once.

## Example(s)
- Western/individualist bias: U.S. and Canada show high loafing; collectivist (Eastern) cultures show significantly less.
- High conscientiousness, agreeableness, and strong work ethic protect against loafing.
- Virtual teams especially vulnerable when members are dissimilar or have competing demands.

## Prevention (R&J's 5 levers)
1. Set group goals.
2. Increase intergroup competition.
3. Use peer evaluations.
4. Select members high in motivation/preference for group work.
5. Base rewards in part on identifiable individual contributions.

## Notes on application
Definition match; cross-cultural angle (Eastern vs. Western); which intervention reduces loafing (the 5 levers above). Watch for distractors that frame it as "groupthink" or "free riding."
