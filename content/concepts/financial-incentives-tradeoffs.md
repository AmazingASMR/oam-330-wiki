## Definition
Financial incentives are the standard managerial lever. Slide 15 (deck 09) gives the explicit ledger.

**Upside:**
- Gets people to do the work.

**Downsides:**
- **Less creativity**
- **Less teamwork/cooperation** (more competition)
- **Unethical behavior**
- **Sense of unfairness**
- **Focus on short-term rewards vs. long-term planning**
- **Effort becomes contingent on continued rewards** (if you stop paying, effort stops)

## Why it matters
Slide 18 (deck 09) take-home: money matters but is complicated. Pairs with [[concepts/intrinsic-vs-extrinsic-motivation]] — incentives can corrupt intrinsic drive.

## Key distinctions
- **Vs. intrinsic/extrinsic taxonomy** — that concept defines the *types*; this one catalogs *when financial incentives backfire*.
- **Algorithmic vs. heuristic tasks** — Pink: extrinsic rewards work fine for **algorithmic** tasks (follow-the-formula). They backfire on **right-brain/conceptual** tasks requiring creativity.
- **Contingent ("if-then") vs. unexpected rewards** — only contingent rewards reliably corrupt intrinsic motivation. Unexpected post-hoc rewards mostly don't.

## Example(s)
- **Glucksberg candle problem** — incentivized group took ~3.5 minutes *longer* to solve the creative-insight puzzle than a non-incentivized group. Rewards narrow focus, blocking the wide view needed for creative reframing.
- **Madurai (Ariely et al.)** — large incentives ($5, ~2 weeks' pay) led to **worse** performance on cognitively demanding tasks.
- **Swedish blood donation** — paying women to donate halved donation rates (53% → 30%); offering to redirect payment to charity restored donations.
- **Israeli day-care fine** — fining late parents *increased* lateness by replacing moral norm with market price.
- **Goal-setting side effects** — Sears auto-repair quotas drove overcharging; Enron revenue goals drove fraud; Ford Pinto safety failures.

## Notes on application
**Glucksberg candle problem belongs here, not in pure intrinsic/extrinsic** — it's the canonical "incentives backfire on creative tasks" demo. Memorize the six downsides verbatim. Distractor: claiming incentives never backfire, or that they backfire on all tasks (only on creative/conceptual ones).
