## Definition
A group is two or more individuals, interacting and interdependent, who have come together to achieve certain objectives.

## Why it matters
Establishes the minimum criteria — interaction + interdependence + shared objective — that distinguish a group from a mere collection of people. Sets the foundation for everything else (norms, roles, cohesion, decision making).

## Key distinctions
- **Formal group** — defined by the organization's structure, with designated work assignments and established tasks (e.g., a six-person airline flight crew).
- **Informal group** — neither formally structured nor organizationally determined; meets social needs or common interests (e.g., three employees from different departments who lunch together).
- **Aggregate / collection** — people in proximity but lacking interdependence or a shared objective do NOT meet the definition.

## Example(s)
- Robbins & Judge: a regional sales group at an insurance company — interdependent, interacting, shared objective of selling policies.
- Airline flight crew = formal; lunch-buddy trio = informal.

## Notes on application
Definition match ("which of the following meets the OB definition of a group?") and formal vs. informal classification scenarios. Watch for distractors that drop the "interdependent" or "shared objective" element.
