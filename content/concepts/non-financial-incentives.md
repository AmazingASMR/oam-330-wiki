## Definition
Slide 17 (deck 09) — "Using incentives wisely." Non-monetary or smartly-deployed monetary tools that motivate without the downsides of contingent cash:
- **Pay above industry standards** — "take the issue off the table"; likely cost-effective.
- **Good benefits and flexibility** — keep life from getting in the way of work.
- **Unexpected (vs. expected) rewards** — avoid the contingent-reward trap.
- **Non-tangible rewards** — **status, recognition, thanks**.
- (From pillars 1–3): **autonomy, mastery, purpose; meaningful work tied to end users.**

## Why it matters
Slide 18 take-home: non-monetary incentives are **highly valued by employees and often low-cost**. They sidestep the creativity, ethics, teamwork, and dependency downsides of contingent pay.

## Key distinctions
- **Vs. financial incentives** — money works for compliance and algorithmic tasks but corrupts intrinsic/creative work. Non-financial levers preserve intrinsic motivation. See [[concepts/financial-incentives-tradeoffs]].
- **Pay-above-market is still monetary** — but it's *non-contingent*. The trick is to remove pay as a salience-grabbing concern, not to use pay as a moment-to-moment motivator.
- **Unexpected rewards** — the lab finding (Lepper/Greene preschoolers): unexpected rewards don't kill intrinsic motivation; expected/contingent ones do.
- **Recognition is high-value, low-cost** — under-used by managers because it doesn't show up on a P&L.

## Example(s)
- A manager who publicly thanks an employee for a tough call.
- Flexible scheduling for a parent with school pickup.
- Annual surprise bonus uncoupled from a metric.
- Connecting an employee to the end user their work serves (loops back to [[concepts/meaningful-work]]).

## Notes on application
"Which incentive design is *least likely* to undermine intrinsic motivation?" → **unexpected/non-contingent** rewards, recognition, autonomy, meaningful work. Distractor: tightly-tied performance bonuses. Memorize "pay above market, good benefits, unexpected rewards, status/recognition/thanks."
