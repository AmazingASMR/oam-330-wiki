## Definition
Lord David Owen's (2008/2009, *Brain*) proposed clinical disorder of leaders subjected to *sustained* power with *minimal constraint*. Fourteen features include contempt for others, loss of contact with reality, restless or reckless action, displays of incompetence, and accountability avoidance.

## Why it matters
Names the worst-case clinical pattern at the end of the power-changes-behavior arc. While Keltner's findings show even ten-minute power priming dampens mirroring, hubris syndrome is what happens when those functional changes become entrenched in a leader who faces no checks. It is the diagnostic label for "absolute power corrupts."

## Key distinctions
- **vs. [[concepts/power-paradox]]** — the power paradox is the general psychological *mechanism* (gaining power erodes the capacities that produced it); hubris syndrome is a *clinical syndrome* requiring sustained power and minimal constraint. Mechanism vs. endpoint.
- **vs. [[concepts/power-changes-behavior]]** — Keltner's three changes (more action, less attention to others, less perspective-taking) describe the brain-level *substrate*; hubris syndrome is the diagnosable behavioral *cluster*.
- **vs. [[concepts/narcissism]]** — narcissism is a stable personality trait present before power; hubris syndrome is *induced* by sustained power and can recede when power is removed.

## Example(s)
- **John Stumpf (Wells Fargo)** at the congressional fake-account hearing — couldn't read the room; "Cross-selling is shorthand for deepening relationships."
- **Indra Nooyi's mother**'s "leave that damn crown in the garage" — the toe-holder antidote that prevents hubris syndrome from setting in.
- **Clementine Churchill's letter**: "you are not as kind as you used to be" — early-warning of hubris drift.

## Likely MCQ angles
Identify Owen as the source. Distinguish from the power paradox (mechanism vs. clinical endpoint) and from narcissism (induced vs. trait). Recognize "sustained power + minimal constraint" as the diagnostic pre-conditions; toe-holders as the prevention.
