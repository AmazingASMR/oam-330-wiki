## Definition
Bazerman & Tenbrunsel's two-self model of ethical decision-making: the **should self** is deliberate, principled, and System-2-like; it dominates *before* the decision and predicts ethical behavior. The **want self** is impulsive, emotional, and System-1-like; it dominates *at the moment* of decision and acts on self-interest. The gap between them is where ethics fail.

## Why it matters
Explains the prediction–decision gap that runs the whole Ethics module. People sincerely forecast ethical action (should self speaking) and then sincerely fail to perform it (want self acting). The asymmetry is structural, not character-based — which is why interventions must engineer the want self in advance.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. [[concepts/system-1-system-2]]** — should/want maps onto System 2 / System 1 with an ethical-decision-arc twist: the same person shifts which self dominates depending on whether the decision is hypothetical or live.
- **vs. [[concepts/ethical-fading-visceral]]** — ethical fading is *how* the want self wins (the ethics drops out of view); want/should is the structural dichotomy itself.
- **vs. [[concepts/bounded-ethicality]]** — bounded ethicality is the umbrella concept; want/should is the central mechanism inside it (Figure 5 in the reading).

## Example(s)
- Sexual-harassment interview: should-self predicts confronting (62–68%); want-self at decision time refuses (0% actually refused).
- Clinical-trial-for-your-child vignette flips from "yes, society benefits" (abstract = should) to "no" (concrete = want).
- Marshmallow experiment: abstract framing recruits the should self and aids self-control.

## Notes on application
Recognize which self speaks at which stage (should = prediction; want = decision). Identify interventions that "voice the should" (mom test, abstract thinking, eulogy exercise, joint vs. separate evaluation, precommitment). Don't confuse with hypocrisy — both selves are sincere.
