## Definition
An idea-generation process that explicitly encourages any and all alternatives while withholding criticism, with the aim of overcoming conformity pressures that dampen creativity.

## Why it matters
Long held up as the canonical group-creativity tool — but research consistently shows it underperforms expectations. Slides/readings critique its effectiveness.

## The critique (testable)
- Individuals working **alone** generate MORE ideas than a brainstorming group of equivalent size.
- Primary mechanism: **production blocking** — when many people talk at once, individuals' thought processes are blocked and idea-sharing is impeded.
- Conformity, evaluation apprehension, and free-riding also degrade group brainstorming output.

## Key distinctions
- **Nominal group technique (NGT)** — members are co-located but generate ideas independently in writing, then share one at a time, then discuss, then rank-order silently. NGT generally OUTPERFORMS brainstorming on number/quality of ideas.
- **Interacting group** — typical meeting; verbal + nonverbal interaction; vulnerable to groupthink/conformity.
- **Common-knowledge effect** — different problem (failure to share private info), distinct from idea-generation blocking.

## Example(s)
- The classic process: leader states problem clearly → members "freewheel" alternatives → no criticism allowed → ideas recorded for later analysis.

## Likely MCQ angles
"Which technique generates the most ideas?" → nominal group technique, NOT traditional brainstorming. Or: "Why does brainstorming underperform?" → production blocking. Don't fall for the popular wisdom that brainstorming is best.
