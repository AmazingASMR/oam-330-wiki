## Definition
Slide definitions:
- **Positions** = stances/offers in the language of deal terms. *"I want the orange." "But I want the orange!"*
- **Interests** = underlying desires/needs that **give rise to** positions and motivate the negotiation. *"I'm hoping to bake a cake." "I'd love some juice."*

A core integrative-bargaining move is to **focus on interests, not positions**.

## Why it matters
Positions are visible and look opposed; interests are hidden and often complementary. If you debate positions, you may never discover that interests don't conflict at all — and you'll fail to invent solutions. (The Fisher & Ury "Getting to Yes" framing.)

## Key distinctions
- **Position vs. Interest:** Position is *what* I'm asking for; interest is *why* I'm asking. Two parties can have opposed positions and aligned (or orthogonal) interests.
- **Positions ↔ distributive default:** Positional bargaining tends toward fixed-pie logic. Interest-based bargaining is the mechanism of integrative outcomes.
- **vs. Compromise:** Compromise splits the position; interest-based work dissolves the position by serving both interests in full.

## Example(s)
- **Orange / cake-and-juice:** positions = "I want the orange." Interests = juice vs. peel (or juice vs. baking with the rind). Splitting in half = compromise; trading peel for juice = integrative.
- **Used car (slide):**
  - *Positions:* Buyer "$4,000"; Seller "$7,000."
  - *Interests:* Buyer needs reliable transport for one year, has up to $6K, worries about reliability. Seller will take $6K **plus commits to pay ordinary repairs for 12 months.** Both interests served at $6K + warranty.
- **Texoil:** Positions = price. Interests = good price + good managers (Texoil) vs. time off, sailing trip, future income, health insurance (owner). Discovering interests unlocked the deal.

## Likely MCQ angles
- Fisher-&-Ury distinction; "focus on interests, not positions" is integrative gospel.
- The orange example is the canonical illustration — recognize it.
- Stuck on positions → fail to uncover interests → suboptimal deal.
