## Definition
We are persuaded through two simultaneously operating channels: the **central route** (System 2 — logical, rational, conscious processing of argument quality) and the **peripheral route** (System 1 — emotional, indirect, normative cues that work largely outside awareness).

## Why it matters
This is the meta-concept of the whole module. Every Cialdini principle, every minority-influence tactic, and every ethics call hangs on which route is being activated. Central-route attitude change is *longer-lasting*; peripheral-route is faster but more fragile.

## Key distinctions
- **Central route** — argument-driven, conscious, System 2; produces durable attitude change. Wins when audience is motivated and able to think.
- **Peripheral route** — cue-driven (authority, liking, scarcity, etc.), nonconscious, System 1; produces faster but shorter-lived shifts.
- **They co-occur** — the routes are not mutually exclusive; effective persuasion typically activates both. The ethics question is whether peripheral cues *supplement* a strong central argument or *distract* from a weak one.

## Example(s)
- A pitch with strong data (central) plus a credentialed introducer (peripheral authority) hits both routes.
- An infomercial leaning on celebrity endorsement and "limited time" scarcity is almost pure peripheral.

## Likely MCQ angles
Expect distractors swapping System 1/System 2 mappings, claiming the routes are mutually exclusive, or claiming peripheral produces longer-lasting change. The slide says peripheral is System 1 and central is System 2 — memorize that mapping.
