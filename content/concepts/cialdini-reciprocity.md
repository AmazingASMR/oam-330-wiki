## Definition
We feel obligated to repay others' good deeds — receiving something first triggers a System 1 pull to give back in kind.

## Why it matters
A peripheral-route lever ([[concepts/central-vs-peripheral-route]]). Especially powerful when you are *low* in the hierarchy — giving first builds a stockpile of obligations. The slide stresses being an ally at least as often as you ask for one.

## Key distinctions
- **Route** — peripheral; the obligation is felt nonconsciously, not reasoned through. (Slide: "not always nonconscious — especially in collectivistic cultural contexts.")
- **vs. Commitment & consistency** — reciprocity binds you to repay *another's* gift; consistency binds you to *your own* prior statement. Different obligation source.
- **vs. Liking** — reciprocity activates from a single discrete favor; liking accrues through ongoing similarity and rapport.

## Example(s)
- Tipping at restaurants — partly expected future service, partly reciprocity for past service.
- Disabled American Veterans mailing: letter alone got 18% response; letter + small personalized address-label gift nearly doubled it to **35%**.
- In a tense situation, "give a little at the outset."

## Likely MCQ angles
The DAV 18% → 35% jump is a likely numeric anchor. Backfire conditions are testable: in collectivistic cultures or when the gift looks instrumental, reciprocity reads as **brown-nosing or bribery**, not sincerity. Distractor: claiming reciprocity is central-route.
