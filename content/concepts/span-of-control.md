## Definition
The number of direct reports a manager oversees; determines whether an organization is "flat" (wider span) or "tall" (narrower span).

## Why it matters
A core structural variable. Wider spans cut costs and shorten communication paths; narrower spans give managers tighter control. The choice trades off cost and consistency against communication overhead and managerial supervision intensity.

## Key distinctions
- **Wider span of control** — "flat" structure, more direct reports per manager. Cheaper. Easier communication up and down (fewer levels). Less close supervision per report.
- **Narrower span of control** — "tall" structure, fewer direct reports per manager. Greater control → in theory, greater performance. More layers, slower vertical communication, higher cost.
- **Mechanistic vs. organic** — span is a mechanism; mechanistic models tend to feature narrower spans and more layers, organic models tend toward flatter, wider spans (though not strictly tied).

- Slides: contrast of "flat" vs "tall" diagrams — flat = cheaper + easier comms; tall = greater theoretical control + performance.

## Notes on application
Definition match (wide = flat / narrow = tall). Tradeoff scenarios: "which structure is cheapest with easiest communication?" → wider span. Watch for distractors that flip "wide" with "tall."
