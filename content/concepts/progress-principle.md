## Definition
Amabile & Kramer's diary-study finding: of all the events that shape inner work life on a given day, *small, visible progress on meaningful work* is the single strongest driver of positive emotion, intrinsic motivation, and creative performance.

## Why it matters
Reframes day-to-day management. Big rewards and grand vision matter less than the daily texture of "did I move forward today?" Managers who clear obstacles, set clear goals, and recognize progress generate disproportionate motivation gains — usually for free. Provides the empirical case behind several lecture concepts (meaningful work, intrinsic motivation, the satisfaction–performance link).

## Key distinctions
- **vs. [[concepts/meaningful-work]]** — meaningful work is the *content* (does the work matter?); the progress principle is the *daily dynamic* (am I moving forward on it?). Progress on trivial work doesn't activate the principle.
- **vs. [[concepts/flow-state]]** — flow is an in-the-moment immersive state; progress is a between-events evaluation of what got done. They reinforce but are not the same construct.
- **vs. [[concepts/intrinsic-vs-extrinsic-motivation]]** — the progress principle is a *mechanism* through which intrinsic motivation is sustained day-to-day; intrinsic motivation is the broader category.
- **vs. [[concepts/non-financial-incentives]]** — catalysts and nourishers (Amabile & Kramer's managerial supports) are the lever set; the progress principle is the underlying psychological finding.

## Example(s)
- Diary entries from professionals across industries: good days coincide with concrete forward movement on meaningful tasks; bad days coincide with setbacks, blocked progress, or unclear direction.
- Manager behaviors that derail it: pulling resources, shifting goals mid-flight, ignoring contributions, public criticism — each erases yesterday's gain.

## Notes on application
Identify Amabile & Kramer as the source. Recognize "small daily wins on meaningful work" as the strongest single inner-work-life driver. Distinguish from flow (state) and from meaningful work (content). Spot catalysts/nourishers as the managerial toolkit.
