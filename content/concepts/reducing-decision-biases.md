## Definition
A set of practical "cognitive repairs" for decision-making biases. Slides quote **Gilbert (2006):** *"Because the brain cannot see itself fooling itself, the only reliable method for avoiding bias is to **avoid the situations that produce it**."* — i.e., awareness alone is insufficient; structural changes work better than willpower.

## The slide-listed fixes
1. **Be aware** — necessary but not sufficient (Gilbert's caveat above).
2. **Allow enough time** to evaluate information thoroughly — engages [[concepts/system-1-system-2|System 2]] instead of defaulting to System 1.
3. **Focus on broad samples of data**, not single experiences — the antidote to [[concepts/availability-heuristic]] and to the planning fallacy's "this specific task" fallacy.
4. **Present information in multiple ways** — neutralizes [[concepts/framing-and-risk|framing effects]] by removing the single verbal cast.
5. **Minimize situations where egos are at stake** — defuses [[concepts/overconfidence]], [[concepts/hindsight-bias]] (blame), and [[concepts/sunk-cost-fallacy]] (face-saving "Concorde effect").

## Why it matters
Closes the loop on the module: every prior bias is a [[concepts/system-1-system-2|System 1]] malfunction; these are System-2-friendly *structural* and *procedural* counters. Note the emphasis on **changing the situation**, not just trying harder.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. "just be more aware"** — slides explicitly say awareness alone fails (Gilbert quote). Studies show even people aware of [[concepts/hindsight-bias]] still fall for it.
- **vs. [[concepts/system-1-system-2|System 2 override]]** — these fixes don't ask you to "out-think" System 1 in the moment; they restructure the decision environment so System 1 has less pull.
- **vs. reading-side fixes (Freakonomics):** the reading adds **outside view / reference-class forecasting / algorithms** as additional debiasing tools — slides emphasize the "broad samples" idea, which is the same family.

## Example(s)
- **William Chan / Gourmet Food case (slides):** had he allowed time, broadened his sample of evidence, and not been ego-invested in his original assumption, he would have reassessed instead of escalating.

## Likely MCQ angles
The Gilbert quote ("brain cannot see itself fooling itself" / **avoid the situations**) is highly quotable. The five-bullet list is MCQ-bait as a "which of the following is NOT a recommended fix" stem.
