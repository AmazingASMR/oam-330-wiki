## Definition
Once a person gains power — whether role-based ("I'm the boss") or situational ("I'm in charge of the boss's calendar today") — their behavior changes in three documented ways: power increases action toward goals, reduces the felt pressure of others, and decreases perspective-taking. Mechanism: decreased activity of the Behavioral Inhibition System (BIS) — the cognitive/neurological/motor "braking system." Likely evolutionary in origin.

## Why it matters
These behavioral shifts are largely automatic and at least partly neurological — Obhi's TMS work shows power impairs neural mirroring; Keltner's two-decade research shows the powerful behave like people with traumatic brain injury (more impulsive, less risk-aware, worse at others' viewpoints). Consequence: the very capacities used to gain power erode after gaining it.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. [[concepts/power-paradox]]** — this concept describes the *behavioral changes themselves*; the power paradox is the *self-defeating loop* (you got power by being attentive to others, then power makes you less attentive). Easy distractor pair.
- **Good vs. bad effects** — slides flag the changes as ambiguous: action toward goals = "doing what needs to be done"; reduced pressure of others = creativity *and* rudeness; decreased perspective taking = clearly costly.
- **vs. [[concepts/power-distance]]** — power distance is a *cultural* dimension; this concept is *individual-psychological*.

## Example(s)
- Galinsky's "draw an E on your forehead" study: high-power participants 3× more likely to draw it self-readable (backwards to others).
- Stumpf at the Wells Fargo hearing — couldn't read the room.
- Body remains calm hearing "my mom might die" under high-power priming; controls show distress.

## Likely MCQ angles
Name the three changes (action / reduced pressure of others / less perspective-taking). Identify BIS as the system that *decreases* in activity. Recognize Keltner / Obhi / "mirroring" / "empathy deficit" as keywords. Distinguish from the power paradox (loop, not effects).
