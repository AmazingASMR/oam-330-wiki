## Definition
**Access and legitimacy** = the second organizational reason to care about diversity. A diverse workforce can offer **access to, and legitimacy in, growing minority markets**. Diversity is treated as a *business asset* — a way to sell to, serve, and be credible with diverse customers.

## Why it matters
Reframes diversity from compliance (paradigm 1) to strategic value: who you employ shapes which markets you can credibly enter and serve. The slide's J.C. Penney example shows diversity of store workforce within minority communities driving customer satisfaction and store earnings.

**Built-in caveat from the slide**: no employee wants to be valued *only* for their cultural/minority status. The danger is pigeonholing — will workers from the diverse stores get promoted to corporate, or get stuck servicing the minority market they were "hired for"?

## Key distinctions
- **vs. Discrimination & fairness (paradigm 1)** — THE Thomas-and-Ely distractor pair.
  - **Paradigm 1 (D&F)**: motivated by *fairness/legality*; goal is *don't harm*; success metric is equal treatment.
  - **Paradigm 2 (A&L)**: motivated by *strategic/market value*; goal is *capture diverse markets*; success metric is access and credibility with those markets.
  - Paradigm 1 sees diversity as the right thing; paradigm 2 sees it as the profitable thing. They are NOT the same justification.
- **vs. Better ideas (paradigm 3)** — better-ideas is about *innovation/creativity from heterogeneous perspectives* on internal problems; A&L is about *external markets and customers*.
- **vs. Why employees care** — A&L is org-side. The matching employee-side concern is being valued for one's whole self, not just minority status.

## Example(s)
- J.C. Penney: diverse store workforce in minority communities → higher customer satisfaction → higher store earnings.

## Likely MCQ angles
"Hiring Spanish-speaking sales reps to enter the Latino market" → access-and-legitimacy, NOT discrimination-and-fairness. Distractor: paradigm 1 framing. Watch for the slide's caveat — A&L without promotion paths becomes pigeonholing.
