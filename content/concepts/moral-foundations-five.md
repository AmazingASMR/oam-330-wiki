## Definition
Per slide 16 of deck 26 (verbatim): *"Across cultures, people's intuitive beliefs about what is 'right' fall into ~5 categories."* Moral Foundations is one of the three slide-listed sources of [[concepts/ethical-frameworks-sources]] (alongside faith traditions and professional guidelines).

## Why it matters
Moral Foundations explains why disagreements feel unbridgeable: people aren't necessarily reasoning badly, they're weighting different foundations. The 5 categories are also one of the slide's named *sources* of ethical frameworks (alongside faith traditions and professional guidelines), so MCQs frequently test whether a scenario violates Care, Fairness, Loyalty, Authority, or Purity.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. Faith traditions** — Faith traditions are top-down doctrinal sources; Moral Foundations are bottom-up *intuitive* categories that emerge cross-culturally.
- **vs. Professional guidelines** — Professional codes (honesty, confidentiality, transparency) are formal/role-based; Moral Foundations are universal psychological intuitions.
- People disagree on *weighting* of foundations (e.g., political cleavages), but the *categories themselves* are cross-cultural.

## Example(s)

The 5 Categories (slide 16 verbatim — name | explanation | violating behavior):

1. **Care / Harm** — *"We should avoid causing harm. We should care for the weak and vulnerable."* → **Violation**: *"Kick a dog in the head."*
2. **Fairness / Reciprocity** — *"Rules should be fair for everyone. If you get something, you should give back."* → **Violation**: *"Secretly commit to only hiring people of your own race/religion."*
3. **Ingroup / Loyalty** — *"We should put the needs of our own people (family, country, etc.) over others' needs."* → **Violation**: *"Publicly bet against your hometown team."*
4. **Authority / Respect** — *"We should honor and obey those who are most important or are in authority."* → **Violation**: *"Curse your parents to their face."*
5. **Purity / Sanctity** — *"We should not violate sacred objects or places. We should not degrade the body."* → **Violation**: *"Use holy water to wash your clothes."*

Self-test reference: YourMorals.org (slide 16).

## Likely MCQ angles
HIGHEST-likelihood: definition match — "Which Moral Foundation is violated by [scenario]?" Trickiest pairs to memorize:
- *Fairness* vs. *Care* — a discriminatory hiring policy is **Fairness/Reciprocity** (rules should apply equally), not Care.
- *Authority* vs. *Loyalty* — cursing parents is **Authority/Respect** (obey those in authority); betting against your hometown is **Loyalty/Ingroup** (own group's needs first).
- *Purity* — sacred-object/body-degradation only; the holy-water-to-wash-clothes example is the canonical prompt.

The slide hedges with *"~5 categories"* (the tilde is in the slide text) — if asked "how many?", answer five but don't be thrown by an "approximately" qualifier.
