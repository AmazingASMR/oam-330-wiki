## Definition
The fourth organizational reason to care about diversity: **fostering better ideas**. Teams that are less similar may experience more conflict and take more time to reach a solution (similar = easier), **but** they also generate more creative, innovative ideas than homogeneous teams.

## Why it matters
Operationalizes the long-term arm of the J-curve. The mechanism (per slide): there is no single "right answer" — different perspectives on how to solve a problem expand the information considered. The murder-mystery in-class exercise (which of 12 facts about a graduate student / professor are *irrelevant* to motive) is the demonstration: heterogeneous teams flag and reject different irrelevancies, aggregating to a better solution. **Even living abroad increases creativity.** Slide closer: *"When two people always agree, one of them is unnecessary."*

## Why diversity → innovation (mechanism summary)
- More perspectives → broader hypothesis space.
- More disagreement → fewer premature consensuses.
- More cross-cultural exposure → flexibility in framing.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. Discrimination & fairness (paradigm 1)** — innovation case is *strategic / performance* based; D&F is *fairness / legal* based. They justify diversity differently and yield different policies.
- **vs. Access & legitimacy (paradigm 2)** — A&L is about external markets; better-ideas is about internal problem-solving capability. Both are strategic, but pointed outward vs. inward.
- **vs. Diversifying expectations (J-curve)** — the J-curve describes *when* the innovation benefit shows up (long term); this page describes *why* the benefit exists at all.
- **vs. Group brainstorming (M10)** — group brainstorming literature shows nominal groups beat real groups for raw idea count; the diversity-innovation claim is about the *quality and breadth* of solutions when perspectives differ.

## Example(s)
Murder-mystery "which facts are irrelevant" exercise; living-abroad → creativity research.

## Likely MCQ angles
"Diverse teams produce better ideas because…" → multiple perspectives, not just compliance. "Trade-off?" → more conflict / more time in the short term, better ideas in the long term.
