## Definition
Two modes of negotiating. **Distributive** = "fixed pie" / win-lose: a finite resource divided between parties; the more I get, the less you get. **Integrative** = "win-win" / expand-the-pie: thinking creatively, adding more issues to the table so each party gets more of what they value.

## Why it matters
This is the master comparison of the module. Many people enter negotiations assuming distributive — and that competitive default damages relationships (especially the "loser's") and forecloses better deals. Integrative outcomes are possible in a wide variety of negotiations; the Paper Clip and Texoil exercises show value can be unlocked once parties realize people **rarely value things to the exact same degree**.

## Key distinctions
- **Distributive: 1 issue, guarded info, tense/antagonistic, fits one-time interactions.** Strategy: make the first offer (anchor), reveal a deadline, hide info, display anger strategically.
- **Integrative: multiple issues, open about goals, pleasant/cooperative, fits long-term relationships.** Strategy: focus on interests not positions, soft on people / hard on problem, objective criteria, package multiple options, invent options for mutual gain.
- **Why integrative works:** other issues are never valued *equally* by both parties — trade what you value less for what they value less.

## Example(s)
- **Distributive:** one-time market purchase (30 vs. 60 Baht/kg vendor).
- **Integrative salary:** beyond base pay — vacation, start date, remote work, tuition support, travel, perks.
- **Texoil:** moved from impasse (no ZOPA) to deal by adding **post-trip employment** for the sailing couple, fuel trade, health care, sailboat ad placement.

## Likely MCQ angles
- "Fixed pie" = distributive; "expand the pie" = integrative.
- Integrative is best for **long-term relationships**; distributive for **one-shot**.
- Trap: assuming distributive when integrative is possible (classic prep mistake).
- Strategy mismatch — anchoring/anger/info-hiding belong to distributive; openness/packaging/interests belong to integrative.
