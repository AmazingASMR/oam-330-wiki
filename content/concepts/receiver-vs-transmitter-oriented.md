## Definition
A cultural-communication contrast: in **receiver-oriented** cultures (typical of high-power-distance, often East Asian contexts), the *listener* is responsible for decoding what was meant from hints, context, and indirect cues. In **transmitter-oriented** cultures (typical of low-PD, often Western contexts), the *speaker* is responsible for being explicit and unambiguous.

## Why it matters
The mismatch is dangerous. KAL 801's Korean cockpit operated on receiver-orientation while talking to a transmitter-oriented system (US ATC) that wouldn't decode hints. Same words, opposite responsibilities for meaning — and the gap kills.

## Key distinctions
- **vs. [[concepts/mitigated-speech]]** — mitigated speech is the *transmitter behavior* (softening up to a superior); receiver-orientation is the *cultural expectation* that the listener will decode regardless. They pair but are not the same move.
- **vs. [[concepts/power-distance]]** — PD is the underlying cultural dimension; receiver/transmitter is one specific communication consequence of PD.
- **vs. [[concepts/cultural-power-leadership]]** — that concept covers leadership-style differences across cultures; this one zooms in on the speaker–listener responsibility axis.
- **vs. high/low-context (Hall)** — closely related framework; receiver-orientation maps to high-context. The course uses Gladwell's labels.

## Example(s)
- **KAL 801:** Korean engineer hints "the weather radar has helped us a lot." A receiver-oriented Korean captain *should* decode this as "we're flying into bad weather" — but the captain misses it; ATC, transmitter-oriented, also misses it.
- **Mr. Kim / kwacang dinner exchange:** Korean indirect-offer norm uses six levels of address, illustrating how much meaning rides on the receiver in high-PD speech.
- **Korean Air's fix:** mandating English shifted pilots toward a transmitter-oriented register.

## Likely MCQ angles
Identify which side bears responsibility for meaning. Pair with mitigated speech as the speaker-side counterpart. Recognize the cross-cultural ATC scenario (KAL 801) as the canonical mismatch.
