## Definition
Happiness is not just an end-state but a causal driver of better social, work, and health outcomes — being happy makes good things more likely.

## Why it matters
Frames "pursue happiness" as instrumentally rational, not just hedonic — happiness pays dividends across life domains, justifying its study in an OB course.

## Key distinctions
- **Happiness as outcome (objective quality of life)** — Module emphasizes happiness as a *cause* of downstream goods, not merely a result of objective conditions like wealth.
- **Hedonic boost (short-term mood)** — Downstream consequences accumulate over time (e.g., longevity); a momentary good mood is not what's being measured.
- **Job satisfaction (work attitude)** — Narrower construct tied to a specific role; happiness here is global subjective well-being and predicts a broader set of outcomes.

## Example(s)
From slides — happy people show:
- **Social**: more friends, higher odds of marriage, lower odds of divorce
- **Work**: greater productivity, success, and creativity
- **Health**: greater resistance to infection; longevity gain from happiness exceeds the longevity gain from not smoking

## Notes on application
Watch for the striking comparison "happiness gain > nonsmoking gain" for longevity. Distractors will reverse causality (claim wealth/health *cause* happiness) — slides frame happiness as the predictor.
