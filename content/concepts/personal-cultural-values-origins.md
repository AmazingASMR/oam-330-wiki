## Definition
**Values:** beliefs about which ways of being or behaving are better than others — the **"should"** component of personality and behavior (vs. traits, which are the "is"). Personal values are individual ("I want an exciting, adventurous life"); cultural values are shared ("Around here, we all pitch in").

## Why it matters
Values explain systematic cross-cultural differences in workplace motivation, ethics, and decision-making that pure trait psychology can't. They're also the bridge between Big 5 (individual) and Hofstede dimensions (cultural).

## Origins
**Personal values:**
- Personality and genetics (heritable temperament shapes what one is drawn to).
- Cultural and personal experiences (family, faith community, schooling, formative life events).

**Cultural values** vary systematically with:
- **Geography** — land per person (e.g., dense Japan vs. sparse U.S.).
- **Economics** — degree of wealth.
- **Disease exposure** — historical pathogen load.
- **Climate** — harshness.
- **Religion** — spread of belief systems.
- **Type of crop farmed** — rice vs. wheat ([[concepts/rice-vs-wheat-farming]]).

## Key distinctions
- **Values vs. traits** — values = "should" (normative beliefs); traits = "is" (behavioral tendencies). Both shape behavior but answer different questions.
- **Personal vs. cultural** — same dimensions can be measured at the individual or aggregate level; collectivist cultures still contain individualists.
- **vs. Hofstede dimensions** — Hofstede's framework is one operationalization of cultural values ([[concepts/individualism-collectivism]], [[concepts/power-distance]]).

## Example(s)
- MBA student values: "selfless service" (Irick), "relationships and connectedness" (Jain), "integrity, empathy, family, my Jewish identity" (Salvadori).
- Population density contrast: 874/mi² vs. 87/mi² shaping interdependence norms.

## Likely MCQ angles
"What shapes cultural values?" → geography, economics, disease, climate, religion, crop type — NOT just "history." Distractor: treating values as identical to personality traits.
