## Definition
A **Hofstede cultural dimension** capturing the degree of priority a culture places on **individuals vs. groups**.
- **Individualistic contexts** — value independence, uniqueness, self-expression, authenticity, initiative, personal achievement (North America, Western Europe).
- **Collectivistic contexts** — value connections to others, belonging, harmony, group identity, group achievement (rest of the world).

## Why it matters
The single most exam-relevant cultural dimension — explains differences in motivation systems, identity expression, ethical priorities, and incentive design across borders. Williams's recurring theme: practices that work in NYC may backfire in Beijing.

## Hofstede's 5 dimensions (context)
Uncertainty avoidance · Long- vs. short-term orientation · Masculinity vs. femininity · **Power distance** · **Individualism–collectivism** ([[concepts/power-distance]]).

## Key distinctions
- **Individualism vs. Collectivism contrasts (slide table):**
  - Motivation: "I want to be the **best** in my class" vs. "I want **us** to be the best class."
  - Identity: "I am outgoing" / "Melissa Williams" vs. "I am the oldest of 3" / "Williams Melissa" (family name first).
  - Values: "The squeaky wheel gets the grease" vs. "The nail that sticks up gets pounded down."
  - Expectations: "Every person for themselves" vs. "We take care of our own."
- **vs. Power distance** — different Hofstede dimension; measures acceptance of hierarchy, not group orientation.
- **Aggregate vs. individual** — "collectivist culture" is an average; individuals within it vary.

## Example(s)
- Williams's Beijing/Crunchers Inc. case: individual sales-leaderboard incentives (effective in NYC) suppress sales in collectivist Beijing because employees lower output to avoid standing out / breaking solidarity.
- Naming order: Western "First Last" vs. East Asian "Last First" reveals identity priority.

## Likely MCQ angles
Definition match (priority on individuals vs. groups). Scenario: incentive system that backfires in collectivist setting. Distractor: confusing with power distance. Distractor: claiming individualism = "selfish" — slides frame it normatively as authenticity/initiative, not selfishness.
