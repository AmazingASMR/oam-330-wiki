# Practice — Module 4 Personality & Culture (Session 1)

## Instructions
Try to answer all 10 without referencing the concept pages. Score yourself and use the "why each distractor is wrong" notes to surface fuzzy thinking.

## Questions

### Q1. Which of the following BEST describes the Big 5 / OCEAN trait of **Conscientiousness**?

A. The degree to which someone is curious, imaginative, and drawn to novelty, ideas, and aesthetics versus conventional and concrete.
B. The degree to which someone is organized, dependable, persistent, and self-disciplined versus careless and impulsive.
C. The degree to which someone is warm, cooperative, and trusting versus competitive and antagonistic.
D. The degree to which someone experiences negative emotions and reacts strongly to stress versus emotional stability.

### Q2. Which definition correctly describes the **Barnum effect**?

A. The tendency to seek out information that confirms a pre-existing belief about oneself.
B. The tendency to accept vague, general personality descriptions as uniquely accurate about oneself.
C. The tendency for interviewers to overestimate their ability to predict job performance from a brief conversation.
D. The tendency for narcissistic leaders to emerge disproportionately in individualistic cultures.

### Q3. Per Williams's lecture and the Talhelm (2014) paper, which statement BEST describes the Rice vs. Wheat finding within China?

A. Northern wheat-region Chinese show more interdependent / collectivist patterns; southern rice-region Chinese show more individualist patterns — driven by colder climate.
B. Southern rice-region Chinese show more interdependent / collectivist patterns; northern wheat-region Chinese show more independent / individualist patterns — driven by the labor coordination demands of paddy farming.
C. Modern urban Chinese workers no longer show any rice-vs-wheat regional difference because the original economic cause has disappeared.
D. The rice-vs-wheat difference is fully explained by regional wealth differences, not by crop type.

### Q4. A NY-based Crunchers Inc. manager (Jim) transfers a high-performing **individual sales-leaderboard** incentive system to the firm's Beijing office. Within a quarter, the top performer (Pang Jiayang) drops his output below the most senior salesperson, and total output clusters at the senior member's level. What concept BEST explains this?

A. Social loafing — individual effort drops because contributions are hidden in the group output.
B. The Barnum effect — employees rate the incentive system as personally accurate but it doesn't actually predict their behavior.
C. Cultural mismatch — individual-recognition incentives suppress effort in collectivist contexts because standing out breaks group harmony and seniority norms.
D. Low general mental ability among the Beijing hires, since GMA is the strongest predictor of sales performance.

### Q5. A hiring committee is filling a long-haul truck-driver role. Per the Big 5 evidence on job performance, which trait should the committee weight MOST heavily?

A. Extraversion — outgoing, energetic salespeople-types tend to outperform across all roles.
B. Agreeableness — warm, cooperative employees show fewer deviant behaviors in any job.
C. Conscientiousness — it is the consistent, across-the-board predictor of job performance, and is especially relevant for solitary, persistence-heavy work.
D. Openness — curious, novelty-seeking workers adapt better to any role.

### Q6. Per the Module 4 readings on narcissism, which prediction is BEST supported?

C. Her behavior is best explained as high Extraversion alone; there is no separate construct of narcissism in the empirical literature.
D. Narcissism predicts long-term performance better than Conscientiousness in management roles.

### Q7. A friend tells you their MBTI result felt "scarily accurate" and uses it to pick a career. Per Grant, Williams, and Gosling, what is the BEST diagnosis of why MBTI feels accurate even when its predictive validity is poor?

A. MBTI uses continuous trait scores derived from cross-cultural lexical research, so its accuracy is well-supported.

C. MBTI's high test–retest reliability means the felt accuracy reflects genuine measurement.
D. MBTI's 16 types map cleanly onto the Big 5, so the accuracy is real but inherited from OCEAN.

### Q8. Which row BEST contrasts the Big 5 / OCEAN with the MBTI on the dimensions Williams flags as the "core MCQ distractor pair"?

### Q10. A student claims, "Cultural values are basically just history — whatever the country's past was, that's what people value today." Per the Module 4 origins-of-values slides, which response BEST corrects this view?

A. Correct — historical accident is the only systematic driver of cross-cultural value differences.

## Answer Key

### Q1. **B** — Conscientiousness = organized, dependable, persistent, self-disciplined vs. careless and impulsive. ([[concepts/ocean-traits]])
- A wrong: That is **Openness to experience** (curiosity, imagination, novelty).
- C wrong: That is **Agreeableness** (warm, cooperative, trusting).
- D wrong: That is **Neuroticism** (negative emotionality / emotional (in)stability).

### Q2. **B** — Barnum = accepting vague, general personality descriptions as uniquely accurate about oneself ("a little something for everyone"). ([[concepts/barnum-effect]])
- A wrong: That is confirmation bias; Barnum is about *generic* descriptions, not seeking confirming evidence.
- C wrong: That is the **interview illusion** (Myers, Ch. 10) — a cousin of Barnum but a different construct.
- D wrong: True statement about narcissism, but not a definition of the Barnum effect.

### Q3. **B** — Talhelm (2014): rice → interdependent/collectivist; wheat → independent/individualist; mechanism is the labor coordination demanded by paddy irrigation (~2× the labor of wheat). Country, language, religion, government held constant. ([[concepts/rice-vs-wheat-farming]])
- A wrong: Reverses the directions and attributes the effect to climate; Talhelm explicitly rules climate out as the driver.
- C wrong: A core finding is that the effect **persists** in modern urban office workers who never farmed — values outlast their original economic cause.
- D wrong: Talhelm controls for wealth; crop-labor demand, not wealth, does the work.

### Q4. **C** — This is the canonical Crunchers Inc. case: individual-recognition incentives suppress effort in collectivist contexts because standing out breaks harmony and seniority norms (universalism vs. particularism). ([[concepts/individualism-collectivism]], [[concepts/culture-and-moral-dilemmas]])
- A wrong: Social loafing is hidden-contribution effort reduction in *collective* tasks; Pang's individual output is fully visible — the issue is cultural norms about standing out.
- B wrong: The Barnum effect is about felt accuracy of vague personality descriptions, unrelated to incentive design.
- D wrong: No GMA evidence is given, and the slide explicitly frames this as a cultural mismatch, not an ability deficit.

### Q5. **C** — Conscientiousness is the strongest **direct, across-the-board** Big-5 predictor of job performance (persistence, organization, planning, fewer errors); it is *not* contingent on job type. Solitary, persistence-heavy roles like long-haul trucking are the textbook example where C matters and E/A do not. ([[concepts/ocean-job-performance]])
- A wrong: Extraversion only predicts performance in **interpersonal/teamwork** jobs (sales, management); it does not help a long-haul trucker.
- B wrong: Agreeableness predicts liking and lower deviance, but its job-performance effect is **contingent on interpersonal demands** — irrelevant for solo driving.
- D wrong: Openness predicts creativity and adaptation but has a **minimal** overall job-performance relationship; it is not the right pick here.

### Q6. **B** — Narcissism shows an **emergence-vs-effectiveness gap**: traits like overconfidence, charisma, vision, and decisiveness read as leadership in interviews and short interactions; long-term, low empathy, poor advice-taking, lawsuits, and follower discontent erode performance. ([[concepts/narcissism]])
- A wrong: Charisma drives *emergence*, not long-term effectiveness — that gap is the whole point.
- C wrong: Narcissism is a distinct construct from Extraversion: extraverts are sociable/energetic; narcissists are self-aggrandizing and lack empathy. Extraversion alone is healthy; narcissism layers in entitlement.
- D wrong: Conscientiousness, not narcissism, is the consistent across-the-board performance predictor.

### Q7. **B** — Barnum effect: MBTI's resonance with takers is a *subjective experience of fit* driven by vague, generic descriptions, not the test's objective predictive validity. Grant adds that MBTI's test–retest reliability is poor (~50% of takers get a different type within weeks). ([[concepts/barnum-effect]], [[concepts/myers-briggs]], [[readings/grant-goodbye-mbti]])
- A wrong: MBTI is **theory-driven (Jung), dichotomous**, and forces a binary cut; it does not use Big-5 continuous lexical scores.
- C wrong: MBTI's test–retest reliability is famously **low**, not high — that is one of Grant's central critiques.
- D wrong: MBTI does not map cleanly onto the Big 5 (e.g., MBTI has no analogue for Neuroticism), and its felt accuracy is Barnum-driven, not inherited validity.

### Q8. **B** — Big 5 = empirical (lexical hypothesis, cross-cultural convergence), continuous, modestly predictive. MBTI = theory-driven (Jung), dichotomous (16 forced types), weak predictor of job performance. ([[concepts/big-5-development]], [[concepts/myers-briggs]], [[readings/grant-goodbye-mbti]])
- A wrong: Reverses the two frameworks — Big 5 is the empirical/continuous one; MBTI is the theory-driven/dichotomous one.
- C wrong: MBTI is **dichotomous**, not continuous — it forces a binary cut at the midpoint, destroying information.
- D wrong: MBTI's test–retest reliability is **poor** (~50% reclassification within weeks per Grant); widespread use does not equal validity.

### Q9. **A** — Williams's takeaway: people whose Big 5 profile matches their job earn ~**15% more** and are more satisfied than those who don't match. Reframes career advice from "fix yourself" to "find the right context." ([[concepts/personality-fit-job-satisfaction]])
- B wrong: General mental ability still dominates as a single performance predictor (Schmidt & Hunter, via Myers Ch. 10); fit is valuable but does not outrank GMA.
- C wrong: Big 5 traits are **mostly stable** across the lifespan — you cannot easily coach someone into a new profile in months.
- D wrong: Williams explicitly cautions to fit **all five** traits in profile, not optimize on Conscientiousness alone.

### Q10. **B** — Cultural values vary systematically with **geography, economics, disease exposure, climate, religion, and crop type**; personal values additionally come from **genetics/temperament and individual experiences (family, faith, schooling, formative events)**. "History" is too vague to be the operative variable. ([[concepts/personal-cultural-values-origins]], [[concepts/rice-vs-wheat-farming]])
- A wrong: The slide names six specific systematic drivers; "history alone" misses the mechanisms (e.g., crop labor demand, pathogen load).
- C wrong: Genetics matters for *personal* temperament/traits, but cultural values are explicitly tied to environmental drivers like geography, climate, religion, and crops.
- D wrong: Values ("should") and traits ("is") are distinct constructs answering different questions; the Big 5 does not subsume cultural variation.
