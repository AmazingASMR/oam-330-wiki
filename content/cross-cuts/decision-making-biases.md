# Cross-cut — Decision-making biases

## Why this matters
The bias catalog is a Module 3 centerpiece, but it doesn't stay there. Module 6 (Influence) is essentially a list of peripheral-route levers that *exploit* these same biases, and Module 11 (Ethics) recasts the prediction → decision → recollection arc as biases applied to moral choices. scenario stems lean heavily on scenario recognition — knowing the bias by its mechanism (not just its name) is the question.

## Bias catalog (with sister biases)

| Bias | One-line | Sister to confuse with | Distinguishing feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[concepts/availability-heuristic]] | judging by ease of recall (vivid → frequent) | confirmation bias | availability is **passive recall**; confirmation is **active seeking** |
| [[concepts/confirmation-bias]] | seek/weigh evidence that fits prior belief | availability / motivated perception | confirmation = active info-search; motivated perception = bent at the *seeing* step |
| [[concepts/halo-effects]] | one trait colors all judgments ("exaggerated emotional coherence") | self-fulfilling prophecy / hindsight | halo is **judger-side** generalization across traits of one target; SFP changes the *target's* behavior |
| [[concepts/overconfidence]] | calibration failure forward (Dunning-Kruger, planning fallacy, better-than-average) | hindsight bias | overconfidence = **forward** ("I'll predict right"); hindsight = **backward** ("I knew it") |
| [[concepts/hindsight-bias]] | "knew it all along" — outcome reshapes recalled prior belief | overconfidence / halo | hindsight recolors *predictability of outcome*; halo recolors *traits of actor* |
| [[concepts/anchoring-bias]] | initial number contaminates numeric estimates | framing | anchoring = **starting number**; framing = **gain/loss verbal cast** |
| [[concepts/framing-and-risk]] | gain frame → risk-averse, loss frame → risk-seeking | anchoring | both are "numeric biases," but framing flips *risk preference* not magnitude |
| [[concepts/sunk-cost-fallacy]] | escalate (or refuse-to-fix) because of past investment | anchoring / overconfidence | sunk cost = **past spend**; anchor = initial *reference number*; overconfidence = future success |
| [[concepts/motivated-perception]] | see what you want/expect (Bruner & Goodman) | confirmation bias | motivated perception is **input-stage**; confirmation is **evidence-gathering stage** |
| [[concepts/perception-vs-reality]] | perception ≠ reality — the umbrella claim | motivated perception | this is the general claim; motivated perception is the motivation-driven subtype |
| [[concepts/self-fulfilling-prophecies]] | expectations change *the target's* behavior | halo / motivated perception | SFP produces real behavior change; halo and motivated perception are perceiver-side only |

All of these are [[concepts/system-1-system-2|System 1]] malfunctions that System 2 fails to catch.

## How biases reappear in other modules

### Module 6 — Influence & Persuasion
Every Cialdini lever is a peripheral-route ([[concepts/central-vs-peripheral-route]]) shortcut that exploits a System 1 bias:
- [[concepts/cialdini-social-proof]] — exploits availability + similarity-based heuristics ("people like me are doing it")
- [[concepts/cialdini-scarcity]] — exploits **loss aversion** (the framing-bias mechanism: losses loom larger than equivalent gains)
- [[concepts/cialdini-authority]] — exploits halo effects (credentials/uniform → defer on unrelated judgments)
- [[concepts/cialdini-commitment-consistency]] — exploits sunk-cost-style commitment to one's prior public stance
- [[concepts/cialdini-reciprocity]] — exploits a System 1 social-debt obligation triggered by a prior gift
- [[concepts/cialdini-liking]] — exploits halo via similarity, praise, and attractiveness

### Module 11 — Ethics
Forecasting/decision/recollection errors are biases applied to ethical decisions:
- [[concepts/ethical-forecasting-errors]] — overconfidence + planning-fallacy logic applied to moral self-prediction (the "should self" dominates forecasts; 62% predict they'd confront a harasser, 0% actually refuse)
- [[concepts/ethical-fading-visceral]] — System 1 drops the ethical frame from view (kin to motivated perception); visceral hot-state drives override deliberation
- [[concepts/ethical-memory-revisionism]] — hindsight bias applied to one's own ethical history; we re-read past acts as non-ethics-relevant to preserve self-image

## Debiasing
[[concepts/reducing-decision-biases]] — five slide-listed fixes: (1) be aware (necessary but insufficient), (2) allow time (engage System 2), (3) broaden samples, (4) present info multiple ways (defeats framing), (5) minimize ego stakes (defuses overconfidence/hindsight/sunk cost). **Gilbert quote:** *"Because the brain cannot see itself fooling itself, the only reliable method for avoiding bias is to avoid the situations that produce it."* Awareness alone fails — restructure the situation.

## Common points of confusion
- Scenario stems test mechanism, not vocabulary — read for what's actually happening (passive recall? active search? past spend? future prediction?).
- **Sister-bias swaps** are the most common distractor: availability ↔ confirmation, anchoring ↔ framing, overconfidence ↔ hindsight, halo ↔ SFP, motivated perception ↔ confirmation, ethical fading ↔ visceral.
- "Be more aware" answers to debiasing stems are usually **wrong** — Gilbert says awareness alone is insufficient; the right answer is structural ("avoid the situation," broaden samples, multiple framings).
- Direction errors: Dunning-Kruger goes **less skill → MORE overconfidence** (students invert this); gain frame → risk-**averse**, loss frame → risk-**seeking**.
- Cialdini items often misclassify a peripheral lever as central-route — all six principles are peripheral.
